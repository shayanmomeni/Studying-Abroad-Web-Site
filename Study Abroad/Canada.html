Why study in Canada?

Earning a degree from Canada opens door to a plethora of opportunities. The number of personal and professional benefits surpasses the financial concerns that most international students have. Education in Canada offers an international platform of competition thereby helping you excel in your field like never before. The thrilling experience does not end here. Let us take a look at some of the benefits of studying in Canada.

Affordable tuition fee – Canada brings the best of education for its students with an affordable fee. When compared to other countries, tuition fee in Canada is quite affordable which makes it an easy choice among international students when they are deciding on pursuing their overseas education.

Quality Education – The purpose of getting a degree is to learn from the best of institutions by the best of faculty members. Canada is reputed to offer quality education which is as per the global standards. No wonder, scholars from Canada stand out for their skills and expertise with impressive accolades.

Supportive environment for international students – The country is known for its welcoming nature for people from all across the world. Canada hosts more than 250,000 international students, a number that speaks for itself.

A living dream – The splendour of Canada is not unknown. Apart from its scenic beauty, it is known to be one of the cleanest nations with the best quality healthcare and transportation. Furthermore, the country is extremely safe and politically stable.

Cosmopolitan culture – Canada is traditionally the country of immigrants. Its national diversity makes it a unique melting pot of cultures. An international student is sure to learn about new cultures and lifestyle that he/she may not get exposed to in their home country.

Canadian Universities offer subsidized educational costs than numerous different nations, which implies for the worldwide crowd. Students can save a lot of money by studying in this nation. Institutions in Canada get subsidies through the government, which considerably brings down educational expenses and also makes sure that all colleges stick to the set guidelines.Cost of studying in Canada for post- graduate programs varies from state-to-state but is still far less than other foreign education (US, UK, and Australia). Apart from tuition fees, theCanada cost of studying comprises mainly the costs of accommodation, food, health coverage and travelling.

Accommodation

Most of the institutions in Canada offer on-campus residence for outer-state students, both national and international. Students have to apply for campus residence separately, and though it is not very easy to get into, their costs are comparatively less than staying off-campus. The cost of renting a room or an apartment solely depends on the location and the type of neighbourhood. If one opts to rent an apartment, he additionally has to pay the utility bills like electricity, phone/landline, water usage and also tenant’s insurance (in some areas of housing). In most cases, universities help the students in finding accommodation, both on-campus as well as off-campus.

Food and Clothing

Students are advised to cook their food themselves or hire a cook for the same, as the basic ingredients come at nominal prices. If they choose to eat from a restaurant, the cost might become twice for the former. For clothing, they can either buy from second-hand shops, which would be reasonable or from designer shops, which would be expensive. Cost of clothing does not take much part in the total cost for a living.

Health Coverage

It is mandatory for all international students to get medical insurance before the commencement of the program. Some Canada institutions have initiated comprehensive health insurance plans for overseas students, but that is dependent on the duration of your program. It ceases as soon as the program ends. Some other universities do not offer any insurance policies, and hence students have to arrange for a private medical insurance. Since it varies according to different Canada institutions, you may go through individual college websites for more information on it.

Travelling

There are various ways for transporting, depending on the distance that you wish to travel. Public transport is available in all cities and major towns of the country. For short distances, you can walk or use public transports like regional trains, buses, and subways. For long distance travels, national trains or flights are readily available. Monthly passes for travelling to college and back through these transports can be fairly cheap. To make travel more comfortable, people tend to rent/lease or even buy their vehicle, either a bike or a car. Driver’s license is obviously required to drive your vehicle, which can be obtained based on an eligibility test from the Ministry of Transportation.

Steps on how to apply for a Student visa for Canada

Over the decades, Canada has been in the top 5 list of countries that have attracted students from all across the globe, seeking admission into the best universities. Getting admission into any college or university in Canada requires an outstanding academic score, and a valid permission to study which can be justified by a Study Permit or Student Visa. Every student that would like to pursue their education in Canada must produce a Student permit. However, getting this visa is not as easy as it seems and requires proper documentation and verification. Before we move to the steps on how to apply for a student visa, here are the basic documents needed and the visa requirements in Canada.

· A Valid Passport
· A letter of acceptance from the relevant university
· Proof of financial support and funding
· Photographs of student – Passport size
· IME – Immigration Medical Examination
· Language Test
· Statement of purpose of your application
· An active credit card
· Other documents including any certificates, degrees and diplomas earned from any previous schools attended
· IELTS, GMAT, GRE scores, statement of intent to move out of Canada after completion of course and detailed description on how you would fund your total expenses.
After you have assembled the above documents, it is now time to apply for your student visa. Here are the steps:

1. Check time duration of processing the Visa

This usually takes 3-4 weeks but sometimes the duration varies and needs to be checked up with the Canadian Consulate. The website provides an approximate figure and should not be taken as a guarantee.

2. Apply online or in person

There are 2 ways of applying for your student visa – one is using the online tool wherein you would need access to a camera and a scanner to help you with creating copies digitally to upload onto the site. A credit card in this case, is required for online payment. However applying in person is simple and requires you to download the application kit and guide and follow the steps mentioned. It contains all the necessary information on what you need to do in order to process your permit.

3. Fill a VFS Consent form

This form needs to be filled up with the necessary details and attached with the application form.

4. Payment of Processing fees

All fees including VFS global service charges must be paid in full at this point to allow the application to go through. The processing fee is not refunded to any student. Also, if you have a partner, spouse or family member escorting with you, and you are applying for a resident visa or even a student visa for them, you would need to make payment for their processing fees as well.

5. Submit your application with all documents

At this stage you need to visit the VFS office and hand over all necessary documents andCanada visa requirements along with your application. Pay the service charges that are applied, submit the completed application and get a receipt which would contain a tracking ID that you would need to use in order to track your application and its progress online.

6. Approval of Application

In case your application is successfully approved, you would be notified by the Canadian Government and they would request for your valid passport. Submit your passport and the letter received to the VFS office and you would be offered a temporary resident visa and introduction letter. On arrival at Canada, you would then be presented with your Student visa.
